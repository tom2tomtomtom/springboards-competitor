02 / 09

Who they are

Australian, agency-native, Series-seed SaaS for creative variation.

Founded: Australia (Sydney/Canberra). Three founders: Pip Bingemann (CEO, ex-independent agency strategy), Amy Tucker (CMO, ex-Twitter/Shopify/215 McCann/UM), Kieran Browne (CTO, PhD Machine Learning, ex-Google).


Positioning: "A creative model to inspire, not give you the answers." Their proprietary model "Flint" is tuned for high entropy: they claim 10–30× more creative variation than GPT/Claude/Gemini on ad tasks.

Team size: 24+ staff shown on About page. Real payroll, real burn.


Logos on wall: Wieden+Kennedy, M&C Saatchi, BMF, Cummins&Partners, Jellyfish, Omelet, TRG, marks. Serious agency traction.


Motion: Monthly "Spark Sessions" (webinars w/ James Hurman, Zoe Scaman, Tom Roach, Chuck McBride) as top-of-funnel.

03 / 09

The product

An ideation SaaS. Chat + canvas + custom profiles.

Flint model

In-house LLM tuned for output diversity. Their moat claim: variation in campaign concepts measured by 2D embedding spread.

Custom profiles

Plug in briefs, brand voice, channel standards. Every idea starts inside your world, not a generic model.

Shared canvas

Chat → boards → team collab. Notion-style ideation space. Guests + members. Templates.

Ad-specific tools

Named "Springboards" tools for common briefs. Web search, image generation, prompt-edit images.

Creative voltage

A dial for "how weird do you want it." User-controlled entropy.

What it isn't

Not a bespoke agent. Not integrated into agency ops. Not workflow automation. It's a tool creatives open when they're stuck.

05 / 09

How they win deals

Content + community + creative-authority halo.

  • Founder credibility. Ex-Twitter, ex-Shopify, ex-McCann, PhD ML. Instantly readable to a CCO.
  • Testimonials from top of the industry. Hurman, Scaman, Roach, McBride. They lend authority.
  • Spark Sessions. Monthly free webinar w/ marquee names. Builds mailing list + brand.
  • "Born in agencies. We get you." The whole About page is a trust play against SF-tech creative tools.
  • Book-a-demo path for enterprise. Sales-led on the fat deals, PLG on the tail.

The core insight

They aren't selling AI. They're selling permission to use AI without losing your creative dignity. That's a positioning play, not a tech play. The Flint model is the proof point that lets creatives keep their self-image.

06 / 09

Honest read

Strong brand. Weak moat.

Strengths

  • Category-defining positioning (variation, not answers)
  • Genuine agency-native founders
  • Big logos → social proof flywheel
  • Content engine (Spark Sessions) doing the BD work
  • Clean, defensible brand

Weaknesses / gaps

  • It's a tool, not a partner. Agency pays $45/seat, still has to figure out how to use it.
  • Flint's "10–30× variation" claim is real but shallow; frontier models close that gap quarterly.
  • No integration into agency ops (Monday, Asana, brief systems, delivery).
  • Seat-based pricing punishes exactly the collaboration they preach.
  • Zero bespoke work. Enterprise = SSO + CSM, not custom agents.

08 / 09

What to steal

Five things we take. One thing we don't.

1. Spark Sessions → Redbaez Sessions

Monthly small-group event, 3–4 agency leaders + 1 marquee guest. Not a webinar. Closed room. Content becomes future clients.

2. Named authority guests

They rent Hurman/Scaman's credibility. Our version: Chris Maples for UK/EU BD. Kim Norden's network. Named case studies from Uncommon/Alt-Shift/Collinson.

3. A public POV, not a brochure

Their whole site takes an editorial stance ("sameness is the enemy"). Redbaez needs one too: "AI-native agencies win. Retrofitted agencies die." Put it on the wall.

4. Productise one thing

They productised ideation. We should productise one repeatable Redbaez deliverable (Phantoms audit? Colleague rollout?) as a fixed-scope offer. Not scaling the firm, sharpening the entry point.

5. Founder-visible brand

Tom writes on LinkedIn, Chris opens the doors. Their three founders are on the About page. Ours should be too.

What we don't steal

SaaS. Seats. Freemium. Hiring 24 people. Chasing enterprise via SSO checkboxes. That's their game. Ours is £1,500/day, 5 agencies, lean. Stay in lane.
