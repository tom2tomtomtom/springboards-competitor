What they've got right — and where the cracks are

Fast distribution. Fragile moat.

What they've got right

  • Category language: "variation, not answers." Owns the vocabulary.
  • Founder credibility. All three founders read cleanly to a CCO.
  • Distribution engine: Spark Sessions monthly with Hurman / Scaman / Roach / McBride.
  • Real logos: Wieden+Kennedy, M&C Saatchi, BMF, Jellyfish, Cummins.
  • Priced for adoption, not for value. Freemium works — 120 agencies in 24 months.

Where the cracks are (Tom's direct intel)

  • Pip, April 2026: "This year has been really hard."
  • Nearly every agency Tom serves has tried it. Almost none use it a month later.
  • Product is a wrapper on frontier LLMs. Not unique tech. Kieran is talented but this is not defensible model IP.
  • Weak stickiness. "Good at getting in, not at keeping it sticky."
  • Seat-based pricing punishes exactly the team-collaboration they preach.
  • Enterprise = SSO + CSM checkbox. Not custom. Not integrated into agency ops.

What we steal, what we skip

Their tactics, our terms.

Steal

  • Named-authority format. Their Spark Sessions rent credibility. Ours: closed-room dinner series, Russ convenes, 3–4 agency leaders + 1 guest, quarterly.
  • Category vocabulary. "Sameness is the enemy" is their line. Ours: "AI makes agencies more similar. AIDEN makes each agency more itself." Say it every week until it sticks.
  • Founder-visible brand. They put the three founders on the About page. We should. Tom + Russ + Matt + Chris.
  • Editorial POV, not brochure. A public thesis piece — "Why the AI wrapper era is ending in advertising."
  • One productised entry point. They productised ideation. We productise the Colleague pilot at a fixed price (A$150–175k, BMF Proof Instance shape).

Skip

  • Seat-based SaaS. Wrong price ladder. Wrong buyer. Wrong ceiling.
  • Freemium. Would train the market to under-value what we do.
  • Hire to 24 people. Not the model. 2 engineers, senior operators, then stop.
  • Chase Series A on hype. Only raise when Enero has signed and there's a real reference in the deck.

Risks that keep this awake at night

What could actually kill it.

Product

Token margin unproven. Everything priced in tokens, provider costs vary. If margin per operation isn't positive at scale, the business model is theoretical. See cost measurement plan Jul 16.

Engineering capacity

Tom is one person. Consulting load is suffocating build. Enero's 2 dedicated engineers or Manila fallback must land — this is the actual bottleneck, not distribution.

Enero doesn't close

Joint pitch Mon 27 Jul. If it slides to Q4, Springboards' Series A likely lands first and steals the "we've raised" narrative. Enero closing in Aug–Sep is the tempo we need.

IP leak inside Enero

Russ suggested giving Colleague to BMF outright — Tom pushed back. Phantom architecture is the invention. Engineers who see it could replicate it. IP protection has to be settled BEFORE any deal signs.

Russ-shaped concentration risk

Currently one person is credibility + distribution + capital route. That's leverage and fragility in the same package. Advisor board (Matt + others) needs to broaden the surface.

Category commoditisation

GPT-5 / Claude Opus keep closing the gap on generic "agency brain" prompts. Phantom depth has to visibly outpace frontier models every quarter or the story degrades.
