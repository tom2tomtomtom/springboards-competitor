02 / 10

Who they are

Australian, agency-native, Blackbird-backed SaaS.

Founded: Sydney/Canberra. Three founders — Pip Bingemann (CEO, ex-independent agency strategy), Amy Tucker (CMO, ex-Twitter/Shopify/215 McCann/UM), Kieran Browne (CTO, PhD ML, ex-Google).


Positioning: "A creative model to inspire, not give you the answers." Their proprietary model "Flint" is tuned for high entropy — claim 10–30× more creative variation than GPT/Claude/Gemini on ad tasks.

Team: 24 staff + 3 founders (~27 total).


Backing: Google for Startups AI-First Accelerator (inaugural Sydney cohort). Then Blackbird Ventures seed.


Motion: Monthly "Spark Sessions" — free webinars with James Hurman, Zoe Scaman, Tom Roach, Chuck McBride. Top-of-funnel via marquee names.

07 / 10

Honest read

Strong brand. Weak moat.

Springboards strengths

  • Category-defining positioning (variation, not answers)
  • Genuine agency-native founders
  • 120+ agency footprint = social proof flywheel
  • Content engine (Spark Sessions) doing BD work
  • Blackbird backing = validated

Where AIDEN wins

  • It's a tool, not a partner. $45/seat, agency still has to figure out how to use it. AIDEN is the implementation.
  • Flint's "10–30× variation" claim closes as frontier models improve. AIDEN's moat is agency IP, not model tuning.
  • Zero integration into agency ops. AIDEN plugs into Monday, brief systems, delivery, billing.
  • Seat pricing punishes exactly the collaboration they preach.
  • Enterprise = SSO + CSM checkbox. AIDEN Enterprise = custom agent stack, senior operator, quarterly evolution.
  • Distribution: Springboards rents marquee guests monthly. AIDEN has Russel Howcroft as Chairman — Gruen panellist, AdMission podcast (300k weekly), 25k LinkedIn following, Tenet partner. Distribution engine is inside the tent, not booked one episode at a time.
08 / 10

What AIDEN steals from their playbook

Five things we take. One we don't.

1. Named authority — owned, not rented

They rent Hurman / Scaman / Roach one webinar at a time. AIDEN has Russ Howcroft as Chairman: Gruen panellist, AdMission Podcast (300k weekly listeners), 25k LinkedIn following, Tenet partner. Permanent voice at the top of the AU market, not a booking. Anchor the AIDEN Session series around Russ + one guest per quarter.

2. A public POV, not a brochure

Their whole site takes an editorial stance ("sameness is the enemy"). AIDEN needs its own: "Tools don't make you AI-native. Rewiring the shop does." Put it on aiden.services.

3. Productise one entry point

They productised ideation. AIDEN should productise one repeatable entry offer — Phantoms audit or Colleague pilot — as a fixed-scope, fixed-price on-ramp. Lowers first-trial cost.

4. Founder + Chairman visible brand

Three founders front and centre on Springboards' About page. AIDEN's About = Tom Hyde (Founder), Russel Howcroft (Chairman), Chris Maples (UK/EU Partner). Buyers pick the humans first — and ours are heavier than theirs.

5. Case study logos

Springboards has 120 to point at. AIDEN has fewer but deeper — Uncommon, Alt-Shift, Collinson, Monigle, Emotive. Publish the outcomes, not just the logos.

What AIDEN doesn't steal

Seat-based SaaS. Freemium. 24 people on payroll. Chasing enterprise via SSO checkboxes. Raising Series A. That's their game — margin-thin, cat-and-mouse with OpenAI. AIDEN's game is fewer clients, deeper integration, senior operator margin. Stay in lane.

10 / 10

The point of the read

Category confirmed. AIDEN is the depth play.

The market for AI inside creative agencies is real, fundable, and moving. AIDEN is already deeper than any product on the market and, with Russ Howcroft as Chairman, has a distribution voice at the top of the AU industry that peers rent by the month.

What has to be true for the A$18m/100-agency picture to become the base case:

  • Russ + Chris open the first 10 to 15. That's the ignition. Enero anchor is the proof point.
  • Productised onboarding by agency 15. Hand-cranked builds collapse the margin past 10. This is the operating priority, not distribution.
  • US and EU on the plan, not just AU. US pays more (AUD numbers are conservative). EU needs data-residency work solved.
  • Team of ~15. Fundable, not bootstrap. Round runs after Enero signs, on AIDEN's numbers alone.
  • Token margin measured, not modelled. Instrument real per-call cost before any cost-plus term.

Everything on this deck is AIDEN's own case. The comparable is context, not competition. The story we tell — to the market, to Tenet, to Blackbird, to the next 100 agencies — is about the depth of what AIDEN does and what happens when Russ puts his voice behind it.
